Steve Jobs

The man who redefined how we perceive technology and innovation.

Here's a timeline of Steve Job's life:

This is Jobs Pre-iPhone Success

  • 1955 - Steven Paul Jobs was born on February 24, 1955 to Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieblle. Baby Steven was then adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1967 - Jobs Family moved to Los Altos, California for the school district Cupertino School District, Cupertino, California.
  • 1968 - Steven was given a summer job by Bill Hewlett of HP.
  • 1971 - Jobs enrolled in Reed College in Portlandt, Oregon. He later dropped out of Reed and began auditing his courses. Notably he studied calligraphy taught by Robert Palladino. In one of his speeches, Jobs said: "If I had never dropped in on that single calligraphy course in college, the Mac would never had multiple typefaces or proportionally spaced fonts."
  • 1976 - Wozniak designed and developed the Apple 1. Together with Jobs, they decided to sell it and in doing so created Apple Computer later known as Apple Inc in the garage of Job's Los Altos home.
  • 1977 - Jobs and Wozniak introduced Apple II at the West Coast Computer Faire. This was the first consumer product sold by Apple Computer and it was extremely successful. It was the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputer products in the world, designed primarily by Steve Wozniak.
  • 1984 - Apple introduced Macintosh, which was based on Xerox PARC's mouse driven Graphical User Interface.
  • 1985 - Jobs resigned from Apple after falling out with Apple's Board. He subsequently founded NeXT Inc. This was a novel and powerful machine which English Scientist used to create the World Wide Web in 1990 at CERN in Switzerland.
  • 1990 - The 2nd generation of NeXT was created. This was a ground breaking moment for NeXT since it was the first "interpersonal" computer. The innovative NeXTMail email system, sharing image, voice, graphics and video in email is now possible for the first time.
  • 1995 - Jobs' funded Pixar created the movie Toy Story which brought fame to the studio Pixar. This breakthrough lead to the creation of more box-office hits: A Bug's Life (1998); Toy Story 2(1999); Monsters, Inc. (2001); Finding Nemo(2003); The Incredibles (2004); Cars(2006); Ratatouille (2007)

    • 1997 - 2011: Steve Jobs Returns to Apple!!

  • 1996 Apple announces that it would buy NeXT for $427 Million. The deal was finalized in 1997, bringing Jobs back into the company he had cofounded. This was when the iMac was introduced.
  • 2007 Apple enters the cell phone industry with the introduction of the iPhone.
  • 2011 Apple Steve Jobs announced his resignation as Apple CEO due to his health issues.
